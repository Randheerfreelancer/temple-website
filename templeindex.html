The most revered and historic Vishnu Maya temple in Kerala, Kanadi Kuttichathan Kavu, offers solace for grief and blessings and prosperity to all devotees, regardless of caste, creed, or religion. This temple is located in Peringottukara, 20 kilometres southwest of Thrissur, a location that has been made sacred by the passage of many great sages, and it has a well-known historical significance and is mentioned in various epics. Our History

According to mythology, the sage Koonamuthappan believed that a strong and amenable deity's presence and blessings were necessary for the welfare of all humanity. So, in order to appease the family deity, he did a harsh penance. Before him, the Goddess quickly materialised. As the son of Shree Parameswara, Chathan Swamy, Koonamuthappan pleaded with the Goddess to provide him the Mantram that would allow him to appear to him. The Goddess instructed him to use the Moola Mantram to summon Chathan to appear before him and the Dhyana Mantram to praise him everyday after being pleased with his penance. Vishnumaya Kuttichathan Swami appeared before him as he wished and took the deity with him to Peringottukara. There he consecrated Vishnumaya Chathan Swamy at the place where the Kanadi Family now lives.

Our Attractions

The Below Mentioned Are A Few Attractions Of The Temple.

Brahmarakshas as a Couple

Brahmarakshas are gods with superhuman abilities. They know the Vedas and Shastra inside and out. Such a scholar's earthly responsibilities would include teaching capable students.

Roopakala Mandapam

The Roopakalam is a rite or offering to Lord Vishnumaya in which entrancing and vibrant representations of the Lord and occasionally of his dependable buddy and ally Karimkutty are painted on the ground using a variety of natural and organic coloured powders.

The Sanctum Sanctorum

According to legend, Hanuman and Chathan are posted guard in the sanctuary sanctorum of the Kanadikavu Vishnumaya Kuttichathan Swamy Temple.

Festivals

Numerous festivals and celebrations are conducted periodically and yearly in the temple, where all the devotees congregate to seek Lord Vishnumaya's blessings. The Vishnumaya Temple celebrates its festivals with a variety of rituals and devotion of Bhagavan Chathan Swamy.

Thiravellattu Festival

Thiravellattu Mahotsavam is held on the day of the Vishnumaya oracle in the Malayalam month of Makaram. Three days beginning with the star Uthram arecommemorated as a festival in connection with Consecration Day during the Midhunam month.

Thottampattu Festival

The Thottampattu Festival is held to please Bhuvaneswary, who takes the persona of Lord Vishnu Maya's mother. Bhuvaneswary, the personal deity of the Kanady family, was crucial in permitting Vishnumaya's entrance into the Peringottukara village.
